Our Goal

Create a mobile artboard app for kids that also lets parents turn the artwork into keepsakes to cherish

Research

Methods: Interviews and a Survey


We used: Interviews (5 subjects), Survey (18 responants)

Our interviews uncovered valuable insights into what guardians looked for most in a kids app as we knew the adults would make the decision to download our app. Needs included purchase verification, engating/enriching conent, and a character driven experience


Ideation/Iteration

Purchase Security Feature

Interviees expressed interest in making sure their child could not access the store for purchases by accident. So we crafted a pop-up with a passcode that would keep this area of the app secure

Character Driven Artboard

We utilized our playful logo squid to accompany our young users as they use our simple and appealing artboard. We also plan to take this further and use the speech pop-ups from the squid to provide guidence and instruction for new users

Simple and Intuitive Marketplace

With the rise of ecommerce, our users wanted purchases to be familiar and easy. We kept our marketplace designs minimal and followed industry proven layouts for the easiest possible navigation


First Prototype and Usability Testing

Our Usability Testing uncovered some key shortcomings:
  • Homepage felt unpolished/cheap
  • Drawing board buttons were too close for kids' fingers
  • Some button interactions were unusual or inconsistent across the prototype

Final Prototype

Based on our feedback, we made the following improvements:
  • We restyled the buttons on the Home Page to make them look more sleek and appealing
  • We respaced the buttons on the art board to accommodate young users
  • We cleaned up our button interactions to ensure consistency
